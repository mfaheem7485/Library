FAQ's

To obtain a library card, visit our library in person and fill out an application form. You will need to provide proof of identity and address. Once your application is processed, you will receive your library card, granting you access to our resources.

The borrowing policies vary depending on the type of material. Generally, books can be borrowed for up to three weeks, while DVDs and CDs can be borrowed for one week. Renewals are possible unless there is a hold on the item.

You can renew your borrowed items online through our website by logging into your library account. Alternatively, you can call our circulation desk during business hours to request a renewal.

Yes, you can access our collection of e-books and online databases remotely with your library card. Simply log in to our website using your library card credentials, and you can browse and borrow digital materials from anywhere with internet access.

To reserve a meeting room at the library, please contact our administrative office during business hours. Reservation policies and availability may vary, so it's best to inquire in advance.

We welcome suggestions for new materials to add to our collection. You can submit a purchase suggestion through our website or speak with a staff member at the circulation desk. We consider all suggestions based on factors such as relevance, popularity, and budget constraints.

We offer a variety of programs and resources for children and teens, including storytime sessions, homework help, and teen book clubs. Check our events calendar for upcoming programs tailored to younger library patrons.

You can access your library account online by visiting our website and clicking on the "My Account" or "Login" link. Enter your library card number and PIN to log in and manage your account, including renewing borrowed items, placing holds, and viewing your borrowing history.

Yes, we accept donations of books and other materials in good condition. Please bring your donations to the circulation desk during library hours. Note that we may not be able to accept all donations due to space constraints and collection needs.

Our knowledgeable staff members are available to assist you with research and finding specific information. You can visit the reference desk in person, call us during business hours, or send an email with your inquiry. We're here to help you make the most of our resources.
